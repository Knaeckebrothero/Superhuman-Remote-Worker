# Atomic Requirements (All Batches)

- **TEMP-051**: For DV‑supported processes, the organization must document internal program rules, change‑control procedures, and provide evidence that these rules were applied to generate the electronic bookings. (Page 21, Section 4.4)
- **TEMP-052**: The organization must ensure that source documents (Ursprungsbelege) are retained for recurring transactions and serve as the basis for subsequent automated postings. (Page 21, Section 4.4)
- **TEMP-053**: The taxpayer must ensure that electronic bookings and other required electronic records are created individually, completely, correctly, timely, and in an ordered manner, and that each booking is linked to a supporting document. (Page 22, Section 5.1)
- **TEMP-054**: The taxpayer must implement organizational and technical measures so that every booking or record is associated with a source document (Beleg). (Page 22, Section 5.1)
- **TEMP-055**: The organization must establish capture, transmission, and processing controls that guarantee all business transactions are fully captured or transmitted and that no unauthorized or undocumented changes can occur. (Page 23, Section 5.2)
- **TEMP-056**: All changes to electronic records must be logged in a way that preserves the original content and makes the change traceable (immutability and change‑logging). (Page 23, Section 5.2)
- **TEMP-057**: The journal function must record every business transaction completely, timely, and formally correctly, providing a verifiable audit trail. (Page 24, Section 5.3)
- **TEMP-058**: If the journal already fulfills the ground‑book function, a separate ground‑book is not required; otherwise, both journal and ground‑book entries must be maintained. (Page 24, Section 5.3)
- **TEMP-059**: The accounting (Kontenfunktion) must ensure that transactions are ordered by account, that opening‑balance entries and closing‑balance entries are included, and that accounts are continuously updated with sums or balances. (Page 25, Section 5.4)
- **TEMP-060**: The taxpayer must establish an internal control system (IKS) that includes access‑right controls, functional segregation, capture controls, reconciliation controls, processing controls, and safeguards against intentional or accidental manipulation of programs, data, and documents. (Page 26, Section 6)
- **TEMP-061**: The organization must protect its IT system against loss, theft, and unauthorized access, ensuring data integrity and availability. (Page 27, Section 7)
- **TEMP-062**: The IT system must guarantee that once information is entered, it cannot be suppressed, overwritten, deleted, or altered without proper logging and traceability. (Page 28, Section 8)
- **TEMP-063**: All modifications to electronic records must be recorded with a clear indication of the original content and the fact that a change occurred. (Page 28, Section 8)
- **TEMP-064**: Electronic documents and records must be retained for the statutory retention period, remain readable, and be accessible for inspection within a reasonable time. (Page 29, Section 9)
- **TEMP-065**: The organization must ensure that electronic documents are stored with a unique, traceable index and that the link between the index and the document is maintained throughout the retention period. (Page 30, Section 9.1)
- **TEMP-066**: The reduction of existing machine‑readable evaluation by converting file formats or selecting certain retention forms is not permitted. (Page 31, Section 9.1)
- **TEMP-067**: When paper documents are scanned, the resulting electronic document must be stored such that a reproduction matches the original image when made readable. (Page 32, Section 9.2)
- **TEMP-068**: If OCR is applied, the full‑text information must be retained, verified, and made available for audit throughout the retention period. (Page 32, Section 9.2)
- **TEMP-069**: Incoming electronic business letters and accounting documents must be retained in the format in which they were received; conversion is allowed only if machine‑readability is not impaired and no content changes occur. (Page 32, Section 9.2)
- **TEMP-070**: Documents generated by the DV system must be retained in their original format. (Page 32, Section 9.2)
- **TEMP-071**: When using cryptographic techniques, encrypted documents must be available in decrypted form within the DV system. (Page 33, Section 9.2)
- **TEMP-072**: Signature verification keys must be retained as long as signed documents are retained. (Page 33, Section 9.2)
- **TEMP-073**: For file format conversions, both the original and converted versions must be archived, linked to the same index, and the converted version clearly marked. (Page 33, Section 9.2)
- **TEMP-074**: The organization must create an instruction defining who may capture documents. (Page 34, Section 9.3)
- **TEMP-075**: The organization must create an instruction defining when capture occurs. (Page 34, Section 9.3)
- **TEMP-076**: The organization must create an instruction defining which documents are captured. (Page 34, Section 9.3)
- **TEMP-077**: The organization must create an instruction defining quality‑control procedures for document capture. (Page 34, Section 9.3)
- **TEMP-078**: Full‑color reproduction is required when color has evidentiary function (e.g., red ink for negative amounts). (Page 34, Section 9.3)
- **TEMP-079**: After electronic capture, further processing must be performed only on the electronic document; the paper original must be removed from the workflow. (Page 34, Section 9.3)
- **TEMP-080**: If paper documents are processed after capture, they must be re‑scanned and linked to the original electronic version via a common index. (Page 34, Section 9.3)
- **TEMP-081**: Paper documents may be destroyed after electronic capture unless statutory or tax law requires retention of the original. (Page 34, Section 9.3)
- **TEMP-082**: The omission of the paper original must not impair traceability or auditability. (Page 34, Section 9.3)
- **TEMP-083**: When migrating data to a new system, all recording‑ and retention‑obligatory data (including metadata, master data, transaction data, and links) must be transferred quantitatively and qualitatively equivalent. (Page 36, Section 9.4)
- **TEMP-084**: During data migration, only format changes (e.g., date or currency format) are allowed; no substantive content changes may be made, and any changes must be documented. (Page 36, Section 9.4)
- **TEMP-085**: Reorganization of OCR databases is permissible provided the underlying electronic documents remain unchanged and the OCR‑derived information is retained qualitatively and quantitatively. (Page 36, Section 9.4)
- **TEMP-086**: The new system, archive, or alternative system must enable the same evaluations of recording‑ and retention‑obligatory data as the original system. (Page 36, Section 9.4)
- **TEMP-087**: If the new system cannot meet the above requirement, the original hardware and software must be retained for the entire retention period. (Page 36, Section 9.4)
- **TEMP-088**: Data extracts, reports, or print files are not permissible as a basis for retention unless all recording‑ and retention‑obligatory data are transferred. (Page 36, Section 9.4)
- **TEMP-089**: DV‑supported bookkeeping must remain retroactively and prospectively verifiable for the entire retention period. (Page 37, Section 10)
- **TEMP-090**: All other electronic records subject to retention obligations must likewise be retroactively and prospectively verifiable. (Page 37, Section 10)
- **TEMP-091**: For each DV system, a comprehensible, structured procedural documentation must be maintained, covering content, structure, and workflow. (Page 38, Section 10.1)
- **TEMP-092**: Procedural documentation must be versioned and historically traceable, with changes recorded and identifiable. (Page 38, Section 10.1)
- **TEMP-093**: Procedural documentation must demonstrate compliance with §§ 145‑147 AO and related requirements. (Page 38, Section 10.1)
- **TEMP-094**: The tax authority may request tools to make electronic documents readable; the taxpayer must provide such tools at its own cost. (Page 39, Section 10.2)
- **TEMP-095**: Electronic documents must remain screen‑readable for the entire retention period. (Page 39, Section 10.2)
- **TEMP-096**: The tax authority has the right to access data in accordance with § 147 AO and related provisions. (Page 40, Section 11)
- **TEMP-097**: The taxpayer must make all recording‑ and retention‑obligatory data available for the tax authority’s data‑access methods. (Page 40, Section 11)
- **TEMP-098**: Procedural documentation required for a comprehensive system overview must be available to the tax authority upon request. (Page 40, Section 11)
- **TEMP-099**: The taxpayer must identify all DV systems containing relevant data and ensure they are accessible for data‑access purposes. (Page 40, Section 11)
- **TEMP-100**: If data are not initially provided, the tax authority may demand later provision of the relevant data. (Page 40, Section 11)
- **TEMP-101**: The taxpayer must organize data so that the auditor can access only the recording‑ and retention‑obligatory data, using appropriate access restrictions or digital redaction where necessary. (Page 41, Section 41‑42)
- **TEMP-102**: The tax authority does not accept reports or print files that present only selected data fields or records and omit any recording‑ and retention‑obligatory data. (Page 41, Section 41‑42)
- **TEMP-103**: For immediate (direct) data access, the taxpayer must provide the auditor with the necessary tools and instructions for read‑only access, granting access to all recording‑ and retention‑obligatory data and the system’s evaluation functions (e.g., filtering, sorting). (Page 41, Section 41‑42)
- **TEMP-104**: The taxpayer must ensure the immutability of the data set and the DV system when accessed by the tax authority. (Page 41, Section 41‑42)
- **TEMP-105**: For indirect (mediated) data access, the taxpayer must supply the required hardware, software, and knowledgeable personnel to assist the auditor, with the scope of assistance proportional to the company’s size and circumstances. (Page 41, Section 41‑42)
- **TEMP-106**: When providing data carriers, the taxpayer must deliver all stored documents and the accompanying structural information (file origin, file structure, data fields, character sets, internal and external links) in a machine‑readable format, including data held by third parties. (Page 41‑42, Section 41‑42)
- **TEMP-107**: The structural information required for data carrier hand‑over must be supplied in machine‑readable form. (Page 42, Section 42)
- **TEMP-108**: If the delivered data are incomplete or inaccurate, the tax authority may request additional carriers containing complete and correct data. (Page 42, Section 42)
- **TEMP-109**: The tax authority must be able to read the data without installing foreign software on its computers; any decryption must be performed before the data are transferred to the authority. (Page 42, Section 42)
- **TEMP-110**: The taxpayer must bear all costs associated with providing the tools, hardware, software, and other resources necessary for the tax authority’s data‑access methods. (Page 42, Section 42)
- **TEMP-111**: The taxpayer must make available the Digital Interface for the Tax Administration for Cash Register Systems (DSFinV‑K) for download, as referenced on the Federal Ministry of Finance website. (Page 42, Section 42)
- **TEMP-112**: Positive attestations (certificates) regarding the conformity of DV‑supported bookkeeping systems are not issued by the tax authority and have no binding effect; they may be used by the taxpayer as decision criteria but do not guarantee compliance. (Page 43, Section 12)
- **TEMP-113**: The BMF writing of 1 February 1984 remains unaffected; its principles apply to tax periods beginning after 31 December 2019, and may also be applied to periods ending before 1 January 2020. (Page 43‑44, Section 13)
