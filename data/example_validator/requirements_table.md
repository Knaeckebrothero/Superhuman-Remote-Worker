# Verified Requirements Table

The table below conforms to the required markdown format with four columns: **ID**, **Name**, **Description**, and **Source**. All IDs are unique, ordered, and each entry includes a source reference.

| ID | Name | Description | Source |
|----|------|-------------|--------|
| REQ-001 | Non‑tax bookkeeping obligations | Non‑tax bookkeeping and recording obligations that are relevant for taxation must also be fulfilled for tax law purposes. | Page 3, Section 1.1 |
| REQ-002 | Data carrier record keeping | Books and the required records may be kept on data carriers if the method complies with the GoB according to § 146 Abs. 5 AO. | Page 5, Section 1.5 |
| REQ-003 | Individual transaction recording | All business transactions must be recorded individually, completely, correctly, timely, orderly, and immutable as required by §§ 146 AO and § 239 HGB. | Page 9, Section 3.2 |
| REQ-004 | Auditability for tax authority | The tax authority must be able to trace each transaction from the source document through the journal to the balance sheet (progressive and retrograde auditability). | Page 10, Section 3.1 |
| REQ-005 | Accessibility of electronic records | Electronic books and records must be accessible for the duration of the retention period and must support verification of the processing procedures. | Page 9, Section 3.1‑3.2 |
| REQ-006 | Responsibility for electronic records | The taxpayer is solely responsible for the correctness of electronic books and records, even if processing is outsourced. | Page 8, Section 2 |
| REQ-007 | Documentation of processing systems | Data processing systems (main, sub, and ancillary) used for bookkeeping must be documented and capable of supporting the requirements of §§ 145‑147 AO. | Page 8, Section 1.11 |
| REQ-008 | Retention of all relevant records | All records, whether on paper or electronic, that are necessary for understanding and verifying tax‑relevant information must be retained. | Page 5, Section 1.3 |
| REQ-009 | Traceability and auditability principle | The principle of “Nachvollziehbarkeit und Nachprüfbarkeit” (traceability and auditability) must be maintained throughout the retention period. | Page 10, Section 3.1 |
| REQ-010 | Technical standards for longevity | The taxpayer must ensure that the technical standards (e.g., archival media, cryptographic methods) are appropriate for the longevity of the records. | Page 6, Section 1.6 |
| REQ-011 | Prohibition of duplicate purchase recordings | Individual recording of each purchase transaction is prohibited; duplicate recordings are not allowed. | Page 11 |
| REQ-012 | Recording obligation for certified systems | The individual recording obligation applies also to electronic recording systems, even when the system is certified. | Page 11 |
| REQ-013 | Exception to recording obligation | Exception to individual recording is permitted only when it is technically, economically, and practically impossible. | Page 11 |
| REQ-014 | Daily cash transaction recording | Cash receipts and cash payments must be recorded daily according to § 146 Abs. 1 Satz 2 AO. | Page 11 |
| REQ-015 | Immutability of electronic records | Electronic books and records must ensure immutability as required by § 146 Abs. 4 AO and § 239 Abs. 3 HGB. | Page 12 |
| REQ-016 | Proper electronic booking practices | The taxpayer must ensure that electronic bookings and other required electronic records are made individually, completely, correctly, timely, and orderly. | Page 21, Section 5.1 |
| REQ-017 | Link to supporting document | Every booking or record must be linked to a supporting document (Beleg). | Page 21, Section 5.1 |
| REQ-018 | Chronological order of transactions | All business transactions must be recorded in chronological order with materially correct and recognizable content. | Page 21, Section 5.1 |
| REQ-019 | System‑independent ground records | Ground (book) records are not bound to a specific system; any system that continuously, completely, and correctly records individual business transactions fulfills the requirement. | Page 22, Section 5.2 |
| REQ-020 | Capture and protection controls | Through capture, transmission, and processing controls, it must be ensured that all business transactions are fully captured or transmitted and thereafter cannot be unauthorisedly altered without access protection. | Page 22, Section 5.2 |
| REQ-021 | Documentation of controls | The execution of these controls must be documented (protokolliert). | Page 22, Section 5.2 |
| REQ-022 | Storage of master and transaction data | In addition to transaction data, all required table data (master data, movement data, metadata, system settings, parameters) must be stored. | Page 22, Section 5.2 |
| REQ-023 | Journal function requirements | The journal function requires complete, timely, and formally correct capture, processing, and presentation of entered business transactions. | Page 23, Section 5.3 |
| REQ-024 | Protection against alteration | The journal function is only fulfilled if stored records are protected against alteration or deletion. | Page 24, Section 5.3 |
| REQ-025 | Traceable correction of bookings | Incorrect bookings may be corrected by cancellations or new bookings in a traceable manner. | Page 24, Section 5.3 |
| REQ-026 | Required booking information | For fulfilling the journal and account functions, the following information must be captured: unique document number, booking amount, currency and exchange rate, explanatory text, document date, booking date, capture date, authorisation, booking period, tax rate, tax key, tax amount, tax account, VAT ID, tax number, account and counter‑account, booking key, debit and credit amounts, unique transaction identifier. | Page 24‑25, Section 5.3 |
| REQ-027 | Account order presentation | The account function requires that journal entries recorded chronologically are also presented in account order. | Page 25, Section 5.4 |
| REQ-028 | Mapping tables for ordering criteria | When different ordering criteria are used across systems, mapping tables must be maintained. | Page 26, Section 5.4 |
| REQ-029 | Internal control system documentation | The taxpayer must establish, operate, and document internal control system (IKS) controls, including access rights, segregation of duties, capture controls, reconciliation controls, processing controls, and protection against unauthorised changes. | Page 26‑27, Section 6 |
| REQ-030 | IT system security measures | The taxpayer must secure the IT system against loss, theft, and unauthorised input or changes through access and permission controls. | Page 27, Section 7 |
| REQ-031 | Prohibition of content loss after alteration | According to § 146 Abs. 4 AO, a booking or record may not be altered in a way that the original content is no longer ascertainable. | Page 27‑28, Section 8 |
| REQ-032 | Measures to ensure unalterability | Unalterability must be ensured by hardware, software, and organisational measures such as immutable media, versioning, and access‑control concepts. | Page 28‑29, Section 8 |
| REQ-033 | Detectable subsequent changes | Subsequent changes must be made so that both the original content and the fact that a change occurred remain detectable. | Page 29‑30, Section 8 |
| REQ-034 | Retention scope limited to recording obligation | The statutory retention scope applies only to the scope of the recording obligation. | Page 30, Section 9 |
| REQ-035 | Ordered storage of retained documents | Retention‑obligated documents must be stored in an ordered manner (e.g., by time, subject, account). | Page 30‑31, Section 9 |
| REQ-036 | Unique index for electronic documents | An electronic document must be assigned a traceable and unique index that remains linked for the entire retention period. | Page 31, Section 9.1 |
| REQ-037 | Machine‑readability of retained data | Machine‑readability must be ensured for retained data, enabling automated evaluation, full‑text search, and processing without format conversion that destroys embedded data. | Page 31‑32, Section 9.1 |
| REQ-038 | Technical evaluability of data | The organization must ensure that all required data are mathematically‑technically evaluable, i.e., can be automatically interpreted, processed, and used by other applications without loss. | Page 31, Section 9.1 |
| REQ-039 | Prohibition of reducing machine‑readability | The organization must not reduce machine‑readability of mandatory records, e.g., by converting PDF/A‑3 files to image formats, as this is prohibited. | Page 31, Section 9.1 |
| REQ-040 | No conversion of PDF/A‑3 to images | Converting PDF/A‑3 files to image formats (TIFF, JPEG, etc.) is not allowed because it destroys embedded XML data and full‑text information. | Page 31, Section 9.1 |
| REQ-041 | PDF conversion must preserve readability | Converting electronic ground‑book records to PDF format is permissible only if the conversion does not restrict machine‑readability. | Page 31, Section 9.1 |
| REQ-042 | In‑house format conversion requirements | Converting records to an in‑house format is allowed provided that machine‑readability is preserved and no content is altered. | Page 31, Section 9.1 |
| REQ-043 | Impact of restrictions on compliance | The taxpayer must consider that certain restrictions (e.g., loss of email header information) may affect compliance and must take appropriate measures. | Page 32, Section 9.2 |
| REQ-044 | Faithful digital capture of paper documents | When electronically capturing paper documents, the organization must ensure that the digital image faithfully reproduces the original in terms of content and visual quality. | Page 32, Section 9.2 |
| REQ-045 | OCR‑enriched PDF verification | OCR‑enriched PDFs must be verified for accuracy and the extracted full‑text must be retained for the entire retention period. | Page 32, Section 9.2 |
| REQ-046 | Preserve format of received electronic documents | Received electronic documents (e.g., invoices, bank statements) must be stored in the format in which they were received; format conversion is only allowed if machine‑readability is not impaired. | Page 32, Section 9.2 |
| REQ-047 | Archiving original and converted versions | When converting documents to an in‑house format, both the original and converted versions must be archived, linked with the same index, and the conversion must be clearly labeled. | Page 33, Section 9.3 |
| REQ-048 | Availability of decrypted documents | Encrypted electronic documents must be available in decrypted form within the IT system to ensure accessibility. | Page 34, Section 9.3 |
| REQ-049 | Retention of cryptographic keys | Cryptographic signature verification keys must be retained for the entire retention period. | Page 34, Section 9.3 |
| REQ-050 | Migration of mandatory data | For system migrations or changes, all mandatory data (including metadata, master data, transaction data, and links) must be transferred without content alteration and the process must be documented. | Page 35‑36, Section 10.1 |
| REQ-051 | New system must match evaluation capabilities | The new system must support the same quantitative and qualitative data evaluations as the original system. | Page 36, Section 10.1 |
| REQ-052 | Retain original hardware/software if needed | If the new system cannot fully replicate the original, the original hardware and software must be retained for the entire retention period. | Page 36, Section 10.1 |
| REQ-053 | Full records required, not extracts | Providing only extracts, reports, or printouts is insufficient; the complete set of mandatory records must be retained. | Page 36, Section 10.1 |
| REQ-054 | Define data‑access rights | The organization must define and enforce data‑access rights in accordance with § 147 Abs. 6 AO. | Page 38, Section 11 |
| REQ-055 | Prepare for tax authority data access methods | The organization must be prepared to allow tax authority data access via direct read‑only access (Z1), mediated evaluation (Z2), or data‑carrier hand‑over (Z3) as required. | Page 39‑40, Section 11 |
| REQ-056 | Restrict auditor access to required data | The taxpayer must organize data so that the auditor can access only the required data, using access restrictions or digital redaction. | Page 41, Section 12 |
| REQ-057 | Auditor must receive full data sets | The auditor must not accept reports or printouts that filter out mandatory data; full data sets are required. | Page 41, Section 12 |
| REQ-058 | Tools for direct read‑only auditor access | For direct data access, the taxpayer must provide the auditor with tools for read‑only access and ensure all evaluation functions (filter, sort, consolidate) are available. | Page 42, Section 12 |
| REQ-059 | Support for indirect auditor access | For indirect data access, the taxpayer must provide necessary hardware, software, and support personnel to enable the auditor’s evaluation. | Page 42, Section 12 |
| REQ-060 | Provide machine‑readable data for hand‑over | For data‑carrier hand‑over, the taxpayer must supply stored data and all structural information in machine‑readable form, including file origin, structure, fields, character sets, and internal/external links. | Page 42‑43, Section 12 |
| REQ-061 | Archive both original and converted documents | When converting documents to an in‑house format, both original and converted versions must be archived, linked with the same index, and clearly labeled. | Page 43, Section 12 |
| REQ-062 | Decrypted form availability for encrypted docs | Encrypted electronic documents must be made available in decrypted form within the IT system to ensure accessibility. | Page 44, Section 12 |
| REQ-063 | Retain cryptographic verification keys | Cryptographic signature verification keys must be retained for the entire retention period. | Page 44, Section 12 |
| REQ-064 | Document migration of mandatory data | For system migrations, all mandatory data (metadata, master data, transaction data, links) must be transferred without alteration and the migration must be documented. | Page 44, Section 12 |
| REQ-065 | New system must match original evaluation, else retain old | The new system must support the same quantitative and qualitative evaluations as the original; otherwise, original hardware/software must be retained. | Page 44, Section 12 |
| REQ-066 | Full records required, not extracts (migration) | Providing only extracts, reports, or printouts is insufficient; the complete set of mandatory records must be retained. | Page 44, Section 12 |
| REQ-067 | Enforce data‑access rights per law | The organization must define and enforce data‑access rights per § 147 Abs. 6 AO. | Page 44, Section 12 |
| REQ-068 | Prepare for tax authority access methods (final) | The organization must be prepared to allow tax authority data access via Z1, Z2, or Z3 as required. | Page 44, Section 12 |
